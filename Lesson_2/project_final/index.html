Existentialism ↓

Definition: a philosophical theory or approach which emphasizes the existence of the individual person as a free and responsible agent determining their own development through acts of the will. Existentialism is a line of philosophical inquiry regarding the nature of human existence, centering on human thoughts, feelings and actions. Existentialist thinkers often explore issues such as meaning, purpose, and value of human existence, in addition to personal agency's role in one's life.

Key concepts of this school of thought include:

“Existential Angst” - A sense of anxiety, dread, disorientation, or confusion in the face of a world which seems to have no meaning.

Authenticity - In this field of thought, authenticity is related to the degree in which an individual's actions coincide with their values, beliefs and desires.

Courage - In existentialist thought, courage is the connecting to life with profound love and compassion; acknowledging our inherent fragility and choosing to forge our own paths in spite of it.
